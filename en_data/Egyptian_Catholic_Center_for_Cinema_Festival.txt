The Egyptian Catholic Center for Cinema Festival is an annual film festival held in Cairo, Egypt. Started in 1952, it is the oldest film festival in Egypt, Africa, and the Middle East. The festival only awards films that are distinguished by their high artistic level and whose ideas and topics revolve around issues that concern and benefit society and have a positive impact on its audience.
The festival is hosted by the Cairo Catholic Center.
Its awards include the Film Leadership Award and the Artistic Creativity Award.
